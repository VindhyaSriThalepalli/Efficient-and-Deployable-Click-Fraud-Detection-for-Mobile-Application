Abstract

Mobile advertising plays a vital role in the mobile app ecosystem. A major threat to the sustainability of this ecosystem is click fraud, i.e., ad clicks performed by malicious code or automatic bot problems. Existing click fraud detection approaches focus on analyzing the ad requests at the server side. However, such approaches may suffer from high false negatives since the detection can be easily circumvented, e.g., when the clicks are behind proxies or globally distributed. In this paper, we present Shan, an efficient and deployable click fraud detection approach at the client side (inside the application) for mobile apps. Shan splits the computation-intensive operations of click request identification into an offline procedure and an online procedure. In the offline procedure, Shan generates both exact patterns and probabilistic patterns based on URL (Uniform Resource Locator) tokenization. These patterns are used in the online procedure for click request identification and further used for click fraud detection together with an ad request tree model. We implement a prototype of Shan and evaluate its performance using real apps. The online detector is injected into the app executable archive through binary instrumentation. Results show that Shan achieves higher click fraud detection accuracy compared with state of the art, with negligible runtime overhead.
